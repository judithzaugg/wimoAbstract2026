Title & Authors
Is there a title?

Introduction / Background
Is there an "Introduction / Background" section?
Is the introduction sentence directly related to the aim and not too banal?
Is the motivation / hypothesis stated?
Is the aim clearly stated?
3
Material and Methods
Is there a "Material and Methods" section? also "Methods" or "Patients and Methods"
Is the methodology / study design clearly stated?
Has the sample size of the patient cohort been included?
Are relevant patient characteristics included?
Has the type of material investigated been stated?
Have the tools for data acquisition been stated?
Have the statistical methods been stated?
Have all results been excluded from this section? There should be no results in Methods
4
Results / Expected Results
Is there a "Results" / "Expected Results" section?
Are the results / expected results clearly presented?
Have only the most important findings been included?
Are findings supported with data (not just arguments)?
Have conclusions been excluded from this section? Conclusions should not appear in Results
Are means, medians, standard deviations, min/max etc. presented?
Are frequencies stated? Number of patients with X compared to Y
Are p-values, coefficients, correlations, and/or ratios stated?
Are confidence intervals for ratios stated?
5
Conclusions
Is there a "Conclusions" section?
Is there a clear study-finding sentence? "Our study showed…" / "The novel findings of this study are…"
Have strong statements that over-estimate the work been avoided?
Is the conclusion based ONLY on the present study findings?
Is there a clear statement of relevance?
6
Other Issues
Have non-descriptive words been avoided? "good", "bad", "a lot", "less", etc.
Have all abbreviations been spelled out the first time they were used?
Is the word count within the required limit? Max. 300 words on Abstractor
Is the abstract structured according to the guidelines?
Have language, grammar, and style been checked?
